Here's a brief explanation of each characteristic:

* SI (Statelessness Index): Measures the proportion of stateless services. A higher value indicates more stateless services.
* DOC (Data Ownership Count): Measures the proportion of services that manage a dedicated data store. A higher value indicates more services with dedicated data stores.
* SST (Service Support for Transactions): Measures the proportion of services that have at least one operation classified as a "Data consistency operation". A higher value indicates more transaction-aware services.

For each service:

* SIC (Service Interface Count): Counts the number of interfaces exposed by the service.
* AIS (Average Inbound Services): Counts the average number of distinct consumers of the service.
* ADS (Average Outbound Services): Counts the average number of distinct services called by the service.

For each use case:

* SC (Set of Services involved for Completion): Lists the set of microservices involved in completing the use case.
